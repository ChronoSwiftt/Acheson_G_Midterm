History

Product Text

Alexander Keith's is a brewery in Halifax, Nova Scotia, Canada. It is part of Anheuser-Busch InBev, a holdings company based in Leuven, Belgium, which owns over 400 beer brands globally.


In 1928, the business was sold to Oland Brewery, which was in turn sold to the Labatt Brewing Company.


By the 1990s Alexander Keith's India Pale Ale (IPA) was the most popular beer in Nova Scotia.


Alexander Keithâ€™s products were originally produced in the Halifax brewery only for sale in the Maritimes.


They are now produced at Anheuser-Busch InBev plants across Canada and America.


Recipes for the beer in the early 1900s show high levels of hopping, with large all-malt mash ingredients and no use of corn, typical for beers of that time


Alexander Keith's IPA is only 5% alcohol by volume and lightly hopped, which does not meet the accepted criteria for an India pale ale


At the 2016 Canadian Brewing Awards, Alexander Keith's IPA won third place, not in the IPA category, but in the "North American Style Blonde or Golden Ale" category
